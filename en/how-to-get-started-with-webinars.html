How to Get Started with Webinars

By Kristian Rasmussen

Hosting a webinar is much different than hosting an online event. While you don't have the hassle of renting a venue, and hiring a catering company, getting started with webinars requires careful planning and preparation.

Why is it important for you?

With the recent shift to remote work and an increase in online activity, people and brands rely on webinars like never before. Since the pandemic's start, virtual events are up 1000% and compete for attention with entertaining content on social media. This article will give you some practical tips for hosting a successful webinar that your participants will remember.

What is a webinar?

The term is composed of "web" and "seminar." In other words, it is a digital seminar. In simple terms, webinars are virtual lectures, conversations, or workshops. Here are the key features of a webinar:

Webinars can be live-streamed or recorded
A webinar is usually conveyed live via video. There are several applications for video conferencingâ€”for example, Zoom, Skype, Teams. You can also stream live on a video-sharing platform, like YouTube. You can also record a webinar and upload it to a video sharing platform for people to watch.

Webinars are interactive
A webinar is an opportunity to interact and network. It connects the speaker to the audience and allows participants to communicate with each other. Modern webinar tools have many options for speakers and attendees to have an engaging experience. Including chat functions, voting polls, or virtual hand raises. As a result, participants can comment, ask questions during or after the webinar using chat or voice.

Webinars are a great way to engage with prospects and customers.
Webinars provide a great platform to spark engaging conversations and build relationships with prospects, customers, and boost brand awareness. Virtual events and conferences are excellent tools for generating high-quality leads and increasing conversion. The ROI from effective webinars and virtual events can be much higher than from other marketing initiatives.

Webinars can replace seminars.
Unlike seminars, webinars are interactive and encourage collaboration. Regular video conferences can be too official and less engaging. This article will show you why webinars and virtual events are a great way to make your internal meetings and training practical and fun.

Webinars can vary from basic to advanced.
Webinars are a cost-effective and easy way to communicate your message to the world. It can have all the components of a real-world event: lectures, conversations, virtual networking sessions, round tables, debates, virtual workshops, and much more. You can set the objectives of your virtual event and decide what to include. For example, if you would like to host a simple webinar, your laptop and presence are enough to connect to your customers and prospects to discuss business or a new project.

Like real-world events, webinars allow you to create a more advanced event to host a panel discussion or a debate. They require thorough preparation and more equipment than basic webinars, but the benefits are invaluable. You get to create authentic and informative content that your attendees will remember. Virtual events are a fantastic way to build trust in your brand.

Benefits of a Webinar

Now that you know what a webinar is, let's look at the benefits of organizing one and how and where you should host it. We already mentioned that webinars and virtual events are up 1000% since the start of the pandemic

With quarantines, furloughs, home offices, and social distancing, webinars have become a sure winner. Virtual events are now the only option for many businesses to stay in touch with their customers and employees.

As we are slowly moving to the "new normal," webinars and online conferences remain as relevant as a few months ago. They allow you to speak to wider audiences, reach more people, and get your brand noticed by potential customers. And, unlike with real-world events, with webinars, you don't have to worry about the venue's size or catering. What's more, you can record and share the webinar after it's finished. It will continue driving engagement with your brand long after you pressed the "End" button.

Webinars are an excellent lead-generation tool.
Webinars are an efficient and engaging way to reach your prospective customers and generate high-quality leads. They offer you an opportunity to connect with your target audiences at the early stages of their buyer journey. Here is how webinars can help you create leads:

  • Collect your prospects' contact information via webinar registration form and follow up with them after the online event. Send a post-event recap and keep in touch with your leads by updating them on your services and offerings.
  • Offer valuable insights and actionable tips to familiarize the audience with your brand. Run polls, Q&A sessions, and quizzes to get to know your attendees.
  • Webinars and virtual conferences allow you to showcase your business from a different angle and humanize your brand.

During a webinar, your customers will get to know the people behind the product, service, or message. Prepare and research your target audience's pain points and needs. You can offer actionable insights into solving their problems and address topics that are important for them. It will help you build trust and strengthen your brand positioning.

Webinars are an opportunity to build authority by inviting experts.
Invite subject matter experts, influencers, and thought leaders to share their insights at your webinar. Such collaborations help you boost attendance and create buzz around your online event. Your team members, who are experts in specific areas, can also share their know-how and help you build your brand authority.

Recommended Internet Speed for Webinars
A fast and reliable internet connection is essential for hosting a webinar. When streaming, avoid using Wi-Fi and opt for a cable connection because it is more stable and secure. Before starting a webinar, ensure that your internet connection can handle the number of expected participants.

For example, Livestorm , a webinar software, requires the organizer to have a minimum speed of 5 Mb/s for both up- and downloads. They recommend 15 Mb/s for optimal results. Participants should have 5 Mb/s.

We recommend picking the time for your webinar outside regular working hours, so the internet connection is less loaded with the number of users.

Run a quick test to check your internet's speed to see if it's within the recommended range.

Video Conference and Video-Sharing platforms for Webinars
The demand for the video conferencing market has skyrocketed during the pandemic. Zoom was one of the most popular platforms, followed by Microsoft Teams. YouTube and other video-sharing platforms are also an excellent choice for live streams.

So what software should you pick for your webinar? Continue reading to learn about our top software choices.

Using Zoom for Webinars
One of the reasons for Zoom's popularity is its user-friendliness and ease of use. You only need to share a link with your attendees, and they can join your online event. The advantage of Zoom is that participants do not need to register or login to Zoom.

However, the free version of Zoom is limited to 100 participants and 40 minutes duration. With the paid version, you can host longer webinars with more participants. What's more, you get access to handy functions like voting polls, chats, virtual hand raises, and more.

Livestorm for Webinars
Livestorm is an easy and straightforward solution for hosting webinars. One of the best features of Livestorm is that it allows you to upload content before your online event and then present it live. You can also host a regular "live" webinar. We love the post-webinar analytics that show how engaging your session was.

Using YouTube for Online Events
YouTube is a great streaming platform. Just press the "Go live" camera widget in the right upper corner, and you are online! After the webinar, your live content is accessible on your YouTube channel for those who missed your live stream.

One downside of YouTube streaming is that it lacks interactivity. Your attendees can use a chat on the sidebar of the video. Still, it becomes challenging for many participants to engage in conversation and keep track of the messages. YouTube also lacks a screen sharing function.

Video conference or a video-sharing platform?
If you compare Zoom and YouTube, as an example, you will find a lot of differences between video conferencing programs and video-sharing platforms. A lot of webinars will work with YouTube, but they will not be as interactive. Think through what type of webinar you want to host.

Using Social Media and Your Website to Promote Your Webinar

So you planned a great webinar and now need to get the word out there and get people to attend. The chances that your participants will find out about it organically are low. So you have to promote it on your social media and website to get people to register.

Before you post on social media, think of creating an optimized landing page for your webinar that would contain the following:

  • Detailed information about the topic of your webinar, agenda, and what your participants will learn.
  • The date and time of your online event
  • Speakers and experts who will be presenting with their credentials.
  • A registration form

You can also create a promotional video, embed it on your landing page, share it on your social media accounts, and in an email campaign. Video should include a teaser to spark interest in your webinar among the visitors.

Leverage your network when promoting your webinar on social media. Create a calendar of promotional posts to be shared on Ultify , Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

Create a Facebook event with essential information about the webinar and a link to your landing page's registration form.

Take advantage of posting in specialized groups that would find your webinar helpful. Ask registered attendees to share your posts to maximize exposure.

How to Choose a Venue for a Webinar?

Choosing a venue can be challenging when hosting a live event. Such factors as location, size, catering, and equipment play a critical role in event planning and affect your event's cost.

The advantage of webinars is that you can host them in a venue of any size. However, there are other points to consider when choosing where to host a virtual event.

Avoid distractions when hosting a webinar.
Ensure that there are no distractions or background noise that can annoy participants. It doesn't mean that you should pick a venue with a plain background that might seem monotonous. But avoid any elements that might take away the focus from the presenter.

We recommend hosting a webinar in a separate room. It can be a meeting room in your company's office or an isolated room in your house. It will help you avoid distractions and noise and allow you to set up your equipment and lights properly.

Avoid noise and low lights.
Run a soundcheck before starting a webinar to ensure there is no background noise or other issues with your sound. To keep your webinar participants engaged, you must ensure that they can hear what you are saying. The venue must suit your microphone and camera. Find the optimal distance from the camera and microphone that allows for the best quality of sound and picture.

Pick a location with good natural light or optimal external lights. Lighting can make it or break it when it comes to a webinar. Both insufficient and intense lighting can ruin the entire experience.

Test all your technology at the chosen location, including internet speed and software.

Should You Rent a Venue for a Webinar?
Sometimes the best solution is to rent a venue that offers modern equipment, stable and fast internet, and good lighting.

Live Streaming vs. Recorded Webinars
If you decide to publish recordings of your webinars instead of streaming it live, you must be aware of the pros and cons of this method.

Recording a Webinar. Pros:

  • You can edit a recorded webinar to improve video and sound quality, add or remove certain parts, and enhance it with special effects.
  • The possibility edit the recording means that you have to spend less time planning the webinar.
  • Recording can be watched by many more people, continue engaging your prospects, and target audiences long after publishing it.

Recording a Webinar. Cons:

  • Editing can be time-consuming and require special video and audio editing software.
  • Recorded webinars are not interactive as you can't engage with your audiences in real-time and answer their questions.

Live streaming a Webinar. Pros:

  • Live webinars connect you with viewers regardless of their location. They are interactive and spark discussions.
  • Live webinars are faster to produce. They are imperfect but live. Sometimes it is better to go live, spend an hour presenting and call it done than editing a recorded webinar for hours or days.
  • You have an opportunity to host live Q&A sessions so that you can tailor and adjust your content on-the-spot to the interests of your audience. As a result, your webinar becomes much more compelling.

Live streaming a Webinar. Cons:

  • Unexpected disruptions. If you work from home and have pets or kids, you might get interrupted during your presentation. This kind of interruptions might annoy your visitors and throw you off your game.
  • It can be challenging to find the best timing across multiple time zones. What might be the perfect time for you might be the wrong time for your participants. You can send a recording, but you lose the engagement of a live webinar.
  • Technical issues. Home internet is less stable than office connection. Software issues, connection lags, and other unexpected technical problems can send your webinar off the rails.

In other words, there are many benefits of both streaming and recording a webinar. Luckily, you don't have to pick just one. Video conferencing software like Zoom allows you to record as you go. Video-sharing platforms, such as YouTube, enable you to upload those live recordings once your live session is over so that you get the best of both worlds.

How to Set Goals for a Webinar?

Before you start planning your webinar, set clear objectives, and identify what you and your participants want to achieve from this online event. Sometimes your goals and goals of your audience can be different. So think how you could combine the two and generate leads that convert from the webinar.

Once you set the goal, you can start defining the topic and the format of the webinar. For example, if you are looking for prospects for a web design company, you should not host a webinar talking about interior design. Instead, give valuable insights into web design, tips, tricks on creating stunning websites, etc. Focus more on creating value for participants than on selling.

How to Choose a Topic for Your Webinar

If your goal is to promote your services, it doesn't mean that the webinar needs to revolve around your brand and offering. You will find many companies doing podcasts on their business topics, without directly selling or promoting anything.

Suppose your company does web design. A webinar on web design will be relevant for your clients, potential clients, and other interested parties. Your goal might be to acquire new clients or to improve the relationship you have with existing clients, but the topic might be "10 tips for improving the conversion on your website." or "UX tips that increase session duration and reduce bounce rates."

Towards the end, you might encourage your attendees to get more info by leaving their contacts, and perhaps offer the participants an exclusive deal. In other words, the webinar has to present value and provide actionable insights to your audience.

Format And Visual Content of The Webinar

Your objectives and the topic of the webinar will determine the best format. Whether it is a presentation, an interactive live session, or a recorded video will depend on what suits your needs best. While deciding on the webinar format, you can select guest speakers and experts for a panel discussion if you discover that they will enrich your webinar content.

Define the structure and the content of your webinar.

You can choose to go with a strict schedule and stick to the plan and timing. Or improvise, be more casual and speak freely on camera. If you decide to present slides, avoid overstuffing them with text, and use as many images and videos as possible.

Follow Up After The Webinar

Contact your attendees after the webinar to thank them for their time and input. Encourage them to fill out a survey to help you improve your future virtual events. Keep in touch with them by promoting your future webinars and sending them exclusive deals for your products or services. Creating a series of webinars around complex topics would help you build long-lasting relationships with your participants and create a loyal customer base.
